What this sample demonstrates

A 15-clip egocentric (first-person) capture set covering manual, dexterous, real-world tasks — from making spring rolls and laying bricks to copying a house key and crocheting a flower. Each clip records a complete task end-to-end from the wearer's point of view, with continuous hand–object interaction.

The set is delivered in two modalities — spatial (depth / 3D) and non-spatial (2D) — so the same activity class can be supplied in whichever form the training need calls for.

What we're showing here: the raw capture itself, plus the quality criteria we hold every clip to. Annotation is collected to your specification in the production phase, so the labeling schema and taxonomy are yours to define.

Why these captures train well

The quality bar we hold every clip to — the part that's entirely within our control today.

True first-person view

Camera at eye/head level so hands, tools, and the work surface stay in natural egocentric framing throughout.

Complete task arc

Every clip runs the full procedure — setup to finish — not isolated fragments, so temporal structure is intact.

Continuous hand visibility

Bimanual manipulation kept in frame — the hand–object contact that physical-AI models depend on.

Spatial & 2D modalities

Depth/3D capture for the same activity classes — supports geometry-aware training, not just flat video.

Real-world diversity

Tool use, food prep, repair, craft and construction across authentic settings — breadth crowd platforms can't source.

Consent & provenance

Captured by our own operators under signed release — clean rights and traceable provenance on every clip.
